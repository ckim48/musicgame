About Me

Andrew Lee

In my web development project, aimed to be used at disability learning centers, I channeled my passion for assisting visually impaired individuals by creating a specialized platform designed to enhance their auditory senses and memory skills. The project's core objectives revolved around leveraging technology to train hearing sensitivity through a pattern recognition game, recognizing the significance of sound perception for the visually impaired.

Simultaneously, the platform addresses the importance of memory by incorporating exercises within the game. This dual-focused approach aims to provide a comprehensive learning experience for visually impaired individuals, fostering empowerment and inclusivity through innovative uses of computer science and technology.

  • Senior at Avon Old Farms School with a focus on engineering, physics, and music.
  • Decade-long oboe player, studied composition and AP Music Theory.
  • Started in middle school with a wheelchair control project for the disabled.
  • Engaged with "Another Way of Seeing" to understand the challenges of the visually impaired.
  • Utilizes a blend of engineering and music to create tech solutions for the visually impaired.

About Music Memory

Purpose-Driven Design

Created specifically for use in disability learning centers, focusing on the needs of the visually impaired.

Train Your Senses

A pattern recognition game designed to train and enhance auditory senses, acknowledging the importance of sound perception in the absence of sight.

Sharpen Your Memory

In-built memory exercises within the game help to improve this crucial skill, making for a more holistic developmental experience.

Empowerment and Inclusivity

Our dual-focused approach aims to provide a comprehensive learning experience, moving beyond mere assistance to empowerment.

Technological Innovation

Leverages the latest in computer science and software development to create an accessible, user-friendly platform.
